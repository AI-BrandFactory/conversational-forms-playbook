# The Conversational Forms Playbook

Static multi-field forms convert at 12 to 18 percent. One-question-at-a-time conversational forms convert at 30 to 60 percent on the same traffic. The difference is not UI polish; it is applied behavioral psychology. Fogg's B=MAP model, the Zeigarnik effect, cognitive load theory, and the 4 engagement patterns that pioneers like Typeform proved. Plus the ethical line between a nudge that helps the user and a dark pattern that gets you sued.

## What's Inside

- The 3 psychology principles that make conversational forms work: Fogg's B=MAP behavior model, cognitive load reduction, and the Zeigarnik effect. With the formula, the research, and how each maps to a form design decision.
- The 4 engagement patterns to install: one question at a time (the Typeform pattern), progressive disclosure, Zeigarnik-driven progress indicators, and conversational language. With side-by-side technical vs conversational copy examples.
- The 4 digital nudge categories that lift conversion: social proof, gamification, micro-copy prompts, celebration and feedback. With specific copy examples for each.
- The ethical line: when a nudge is a nudge and when it is a dark pattern (Wells Fargo, Facebook, Vocal Image subscription-trap examples). How to stay on the right side of the FTC and the user.
- The implementation checklist: 20 concrete items to audit on any conversational form you ship, grouped by principle.
- The 8 failures that break conversational forms in production, with the specific fix for each.
